# Brand and Content Guide

## Brand character

Clear, confident, warm, dependable, commercially aware, and human. GSP should feel like an experienced operating partner.

## Provisional identity

Use the current homepage identity until a final logo is approved:

- Briefcase-style icon in a rounded square.
- “Global Staffing” with smaller “PARTNERS” wordmark.
- Warm neutral brown, charcoal, cream, white, and muted gray palette.
- Maintain a text-based fallback so the brand remains usable without the icon.
- Keep the logo as an easily replaceable component; do not hard-code it into page imagery.
- Prepare light, dark, icon-only, and horizontal variants when the real logo is finalized.

The screenshot is a direction, not a license to trace third-party assets or fonts.

## Voice

- Lead with the business result.
- Use short, specific sentences.
- Explain what happens next.
- Use “remote talent,” “staffing partner,” “candidate,” and “contractor.”
- Speak directly using “you” and “your team.”
- Avoid exaggerated promises and artificial urgency.

## Avoid

- “Cheap labor,” “low-cost workers,” or exploitative outsourcing language.
- Empty phrases such as “world-class solutions” without evidence.
- Corporate jargon, long paragraphs, excessive exclamation marks.
- Guaranteed outcomes, fabricated testimonials, clients, certifications, or numbers.
- Publishing `500+`, `98%`, `14 days`, `12 countries`, the displayed phone/address, or any similar item until the owner verifies it.

## Core message hierarchy

1. Hire with confidence and visibility.
2. GSP handles sourcing, vetting, coordination, and ongoing support.
3. Clients can see progress rather than chase updates.
4. Candidates are assessed for role, culture, communication, and reliability.
5. The relationship continues after placement where managed staffing applies.

## CTA language

Primary: **Book a Call**, **Start Hiring**, or **Tell Us What You Need**.

Secondary: **See How It Works**, **Explore Services**, or **Apply for Opportunities**.

Use one consistent primary label per page. “Get Started” must lead to an explicit next step, not a vague page.

## Content rules

- Every page needs one audience, one main promise, and one primary CTA.
- Headings should be understandable without reading body copy.
- Break explanations into steps, cards, FAQs, or timelines when useful.
- Only use testimonials with permission and attribution.
- Label employment arrangement accurately; do not imply employees are contractors or vice versa.
- Use real FAQs gathered from client and candidate conversations.
