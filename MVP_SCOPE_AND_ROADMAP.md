# MVP Scope and Roadmap

## Phase 1 — Public website

Home, Services, How It Works, For Clients, For Applicants, For Contractors, About, Contact/Book a Call, Login shell, legal pages, responsive design, SEO, analytics, accessible forms, and verified production deployment.

## Phase 2 — Authentication and roles

Authentication, invitations/onboarding, user profiles, organizations, role-based routing, server authorization, database RLS, audit-sensitive actions, password reset, and protected dashboard shells.

## Phase 3 — Recruitment system

Clients, jobs, applicants, applications, stage history, notes, documents, shortlists, interviews/next steps, basic notifications, and client/HR/applicant views.

## Phase 4 — Contractor management

Contractors, assignments/projects, tasks, goals, timer/manual time entry, weekly timesheets, approval/rejection, notes, blockers, and client reporting.

## Phase 5 — Finance layer

Billable classifications, payout estimates, invoice summaries, margin reporting, payment status, and exports. Require separate accounting and compliance review before automation.

## Explicitly out of MVP

- Payroll automation or tax calculations
- Automated contractor payments
- Full accounting platform
- Full Clockify clone
- Complex AI matching or automated hiring decisions
- Advanced analytics/data warehouse
- Native mobile apps
- Enterprise customization and SSO
- Large automation engine
- Public candidate directory
- Unmoderated client-candidate messaging

## Release gates

Do not start the next phase until the current phase meets `QA_AND_DEFINITION_OF_DONE.md`, critical security risks are resolved, production data requirements are known, and the owner has approved scope.
