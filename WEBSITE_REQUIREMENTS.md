# Public Website Requirements

## Global navigation

Desktop: logo, Services, How It Works, For Clients, For Applicants, For Contractors, About, Contact, Login, and primary Book a Call CTA.

Mobile: accessible menu, visible primary CTA, no horizontal overflow. Header may become compact/sticky without obscuring content.

Footer: short positioning statement, page links, service links, verified contact details, privacy, terms, accessibility/contact option, and current copyright year.

## Required pages

### Home

- Hero with outcome-led headline, concise explanation, Book a Call and How It Works CTAs.
- Trust section answering: who are we hiring, what progress is being made, and are they producing value?
- Services overview: sourcing, vetting, recruitment, and managed staffing.
- How the process works.
- Differentiators and evidence.
- Verified proof only: testimonials, results, or case studies.
- Final CTA and footer.

Keep the current visual rhythm: dark photographic hero, generous white space, restrained cards, warm accent, strong typography. Improve accessibility, responsive scaling, copy accuracy, and CTA clarity.

### Services

Explain end-to-end recruitment, candidate sourcing and screening, onboarding support, managed contractor support, time visibility, and client reporting. State deliverables, ideal customer, engagement flow, and exclusions.

### For Clients

Address hiring pain, quality, speed, transparency, and ongoing accountability. Show the client journey and a preview of dashboard visibility. CTA: Book a Call or Submit Hiring Requirements.

### How It Works

Recommended flow: discovery → role definition → sourcing → screening → shortlist → client interviews → offer/placement → onboarding → managed support. Clarify responsibilities and typical next steps without promising unverified timelines.

### For Applicants

Explain opportunities, application steps, profile/resume requirements, status visibility, interview expectations, privacy, and anti-scam notice. Never charge applicants to apply.

### For Contractors

Explain assignments, onboarding, expectations, tasks/goals, time entry, timesheets, support, and payment-information boundaries. Avoid implying guaranteed work.

### About

Mission, working philosophy, operating model, leadership/team information when approved, and why GSP exists. Do not invent a company history.

### Contact / Book a Call

Form fields: name, work email, company, role(s) to hire, hiring timeline, approximate team size or number of hires, message, and privacy consent. Keep optional fields optional. Provide success, validation, error, spam-protection, and fallback contact states.

### Login

Secure authentication entry with password reset and clear help path. Do not expose role selection as an authorization mechanism.

### Legal

Privacy Policy, Terms/Website Terms, Cookie notice if non-essential cookies are used, and any jurisdiction-specific notices reviewed by qualified counsel.

## SEO and discovery

- Unique title, description, canonical URL, and share image per public page.
- One logical H1; semantic heading order.
- Organization and relevant service structured data only when facts are verified.
- Sitemap, robots file, clean URLs, descriptive link text.
- Never create thin location/service doorway pages.

## Analytics

Track privacy-conscious events: primary CTA click, form start, form success, booked call, applicant CTA, login entry, and major errors. Do not collect resumes or sensitive form contents in analytics.
